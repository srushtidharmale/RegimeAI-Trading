Key Takeaways for Presentation

🎯
Risk-Adjusted Trading Techniques
Academic & Industry Perspective
Carver's emphasis on volatility targeting and normalization provides a robust framework for consistent performance across different market regimes.
Volatility targeting ensures consistent risk exposure
Normalization prevents strategies from being dominated by high-volatility assets
Risk-adjusted returns are more stable than absolute returns
Essential for multi-asset portfolio construction
Case Study: 2020 COVID Crisis
During the March 2020 market crash, volatility-targeted strategies automatically reduced position sizes as volatility spiked, preventing catastrophic losses while maintaining exposure to recovery opportunities.
⚖️
Robust Portfolio Construction
Beyond Simple Diversification
Moving beyond naive equal-weighting to correlation-aware position sizing and risk budgeting creates more stable returns.
Correlation-aware sizing prevents concentration risk
Risk budgeting allocates capital based on risk contribution
Dynamic rebalancing adapts to changing market conditions
Robustness testing ensures strategy durability
Case Study: Correlation Breakdown
In 2008, traditional diversification failed as correlations spiked. Carver's correlation-aware approach automatically reduced positions in highly correlated assets, preserving capital.

System Integrity & Implementation
From Theory to Practice
Transaction costs, slippage modeling, and robust execution are as important as signal quality in real-world implementation.
Transaction cost modeling is crucial for realistic performance
Slippage can significantly impact net returns
Robust execution algorithms minimize market impact
System monitoring prevents catastrophic failures

A systematic strategy showed 2.5% alpha before costs but only 0.8% after realistic transaction costs. Proper cost modeling revealed the true economic viability.
